Serving from the Sofa

Late last May a friend and I bought a Pine64. If you don't know about Pine, you should definitely check them out. tl;dr it's a slightly cheaper, 64-bit RaspberyPi. Having just completed an intro course in Web Programming, I yearned to serve my own data, and the Pine was to become my first demo server.

The Pine community is decidedly smaller than the Pi's base and there are markedly fewer distributions and OS's for Pine. I played around for a while with a Debian distro, then Arch Linux, but they were both a little clunky hooked up to a display, and they both gave up after about a week. Neither of these distros had a BIOS, so recovery was pretty much impossible, and the Pine could only be brought back to life by wiping the SD card and buring a new image onto it. I finally settled on Longsleep's Ubuntu Xenial image. It's lightweight and boots fast, unburdened by a desktop environment. If you want a home server like mine, check out this excellent guide on reddit.

Praline, as the Pine has been named, sits on the floor of our living room, beside out flower-print couch, with an old LaCie 1TB hard drive mounted as part of the file system. Apache's httpd is my main static-content software, with MySQL and PHP rounding out the traditional LAMP stack. I use express powered nodejs apps to do the extra lifting for funky API calls, like my DarkSky and Sunburst powered content.

Everything had been much simpler early on, when Comcast was our ISP. I ended up liking comcast well enough because they seemed to be OK with inboud traffic on port 80. Our current ISP, RCN, blocks inbound on 80, and won't offer me a good reason why. Awfully rude, I think. To get around this, I have apache listening on 82, and a reverse proxy listening on 80. Now you can get to this site in two different ways: you can talk directly to Praline at wwmairs.com:82 or you can talk to my proxy at w.wwmairs.com. I have some degree of confidence that I'm not currently violating RCN's terms and conditions, but I'm no lawyer. We'll find out when the cease and desist letter comes.
